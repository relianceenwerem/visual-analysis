What is visible

An elevated view down onto a town partially submerged by a brown, fast-moving river. Red-brick terraced buildings rise from the water with their ground floors lost. A Gothic church steeple stands clearly above the flood. Concrete dock pilings are nearly submerged in the foreground. Construction or response equipment sits on the visible bank at left.

Pattern reading

The steeple is the vertical witness in its most legible form. The dock pilings act as a measured scale of how high the water has risen against known infrastructure. Excavators and machinery on the bank place this image firmly in the well-resourced quadrant — a wealthy state response is mobilising even as the water is still high.

What it shows about response

The image carries the same flood physics as image 05 but with entirely different stakes. Property damage will be insured. The state will replace what was lost. The pattern of the response gap (image 01 → 05) runs straight through this image, because here the natural disaster meets institutional response at full strength — which is what makes the under-resourced cases legible as inequality rather than misfortune.
